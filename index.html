Research Experience

Graduate Research at NC State University (2021–Present)

Focus: Developing advanced techniques using Heegaard Floer Homology to analyze low-dimensional topological spaces.

Key Efforts:

  • Constructing sophisticated invariants (e.g., higher-order invariants) for classifying low-dimensional manifolds.
  • Following ideas from Baez and Lurie to construct point invariants in decategorified bordered sutured Heegaard Floer Homology that extend up to 3/4-dimensional spaces.

Tools & Approaches: Methods from analysis, higher algebras, and representation theory.

Applications: Knot theory, quantum computing, topological quantum computing, signal processing, and imaging.

Machine Learning and Quantum Algorithms

Collaborated with the Quantum Information and Materials Group at the University of Arizona. Developed machine learning algorithms using a multi-layer perceptron to design parameters for optical quantum experiments.

CUR Decomposition

Developed robust subspace clustering algorithms (namely the Robust CUR or RCUR decomposition). Tested performance on benchmark datasets such as Hopkins155 (motion segmentation) and Yale Extended Facial Recognition.
